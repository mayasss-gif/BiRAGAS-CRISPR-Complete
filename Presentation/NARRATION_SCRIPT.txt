SLIDE 2 — THE PROBLEM (0:30 - 1:15)
================================================================

Let's talk about the bottleneck that pharma is stuck on.

CRISPR screens can identify hundreds of cancer driver genes. But here's the problem: when you knock out a single target, the cancer compensates. Block the M-A-P-K cascade? The PI3K pathway takes over. Block PI3K? M-A-P-K reactivates.

This is exactly why K-RAS G12D — the dominant mutation in ninety-five percent of pancreatic cancer — has been called "undruggable" for forty years.

What's missing? Cross-modal combinations. We need to target multiple molecular levels simultaneously — DNA knockout plus RNA knockdown — to break these compensation loops. And that's exactly what BiRAGAS CRISPR Complete does.
